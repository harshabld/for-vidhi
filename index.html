who are you
prologue

Some stories don't begin.
They just happen.

No plan. No warning. No dramatic opening line.
Just two people, oceans of distance apart, and a screen glowing quietly in the dark.

★★ ★★ ★★ ★★
chapter one

A corner of the internet
nobody talks about.

It was Reddit. Of all places — Reddit. The kind of app you open half-asleep at midnight when sleep refuses to come and the silence gets too loud.

He was scrolling. Just scrolling. And then he saw her words somewhere in a thread — funny, sharp, a little strange in the best way. He read them twice. Then a third time.

that made him stop mid-scroll.

He typed something back. Hit send before he could overthink it. And waited.

chapter two

The first message.
Then another. Then fifty more.

She replied. And somehow that single reply cracked something open. One message became ten. Ten became a hundred. They talked the way people talk when they forget to be careful — honestly, recklessly, freely.

The thing about late-night conversations is the filter disappears. Nobody is performing. Nobody is being careful. You say what you actually mean. And that turns out to be the most terrifying and beautiful thing.

chapter three

Then came
the calls.

A voice is different. Suddenly it wasn't just words on a screen — it was a real, warm, living person. Breathing. Laughing. Existing somewhere out there in the same dark world.

He'd wait for her to pick up with this nervous electricity — the kind you haven't felt since you were young and everything still had weight. She made everything have weight again.

Hours disappeared. Time did the thing it does when you're somewhere you genuinely want to be.

chapter four

It only ever happened
at night.

That was their whole world — the hours between midnight and 3am, when the rest of the planet went quiet and it was just the two of them.

2:00

She became the last thought before sleep and the first reason to reach for his phone in the morning. He'd get a message from her and smile like an idiot and have to put the phone down just to compose himself.

Not because she was far. But because she was real. Genuinely, surprisingly real.

chapter five

In very little time,
she understood him.

That's the thing nobody tells you about rare connections — they don't need years. Some people you meet and it's as if the conversation had already been going on somewhere and you finally found the frequency.

She got his humour. He got her silences. They talked about goals — real ones, the kind you only say out loud when you actually trust someone. They talked about the nights that were hard. And somehow those conversations made the nights less hard.

Low cortisol. That's what she gave him, without even trying. Just her presence. Just her.

chapter six

And here's the part
he can't quite explain.

He hadn't planned any of this. You never do. You're just on Reddit at midnight being a normal person and then suddenly someone exists and the whole map shifts slightly.

He doesn't have the perfect words for what this is, or where it goes from here. He just knows — she's one of the good ones. The rare kind. The kind of person you're quietly, deeply grateful the internet exists for.
