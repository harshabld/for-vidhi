who are you
prologue

Some stories don't begin.
They find you.

No plan. No warning. Just a quiet night, a random thread, and two strangers who had no idea something was about to shift.

★★ ★★ ★★ ★★
chapter one

She found him
on r/chat.

She reached out first. A message from a stranger, past midnight, on a Reddit thread where most people just scroll through without stopping. She stopped.

✦
r/chat · past midnight
hey... random, but your comment made me smile. hi.

He replied. And that one reply became something neither of them could stop. Midnight became 1am. 1am became 3. Neither of them noticed. Neither of them minded.

chapter two

She had walls.
He had patience.

She wasn't easy to know — and that's not a flaw, that's care. There were layers. There were 2FAs. Doors she didn't open on the first knock, or the second.

He knocked anyway. Gently, every time.

She was worth every layer. He didn't rush, didn't push. He just kept showing up — night after night — and slowly, beautifully, she started letting him in.

chapter three

Till 3am.
Every night.

This became their ritual — the late hours, the quiet world, just the two of them in the soft glow of screens. No alarms. No sense of time. Just conversation that kept pulling them forward.

3:00
the world was asleep
but they were
wide awake
for each other

He was excited — genuinely, embarrassingly excited — every single time. A message from her felt like something. Like his whole evening had been quietly building toward it. He'd read what she wrote twice, three times. Not because it was confusing. Because he liked the way she said things.

chapter four

And then
her voice.

There are voices, and then there are voices — the kind that don't just reach your ears, they reach something quieter underneath. She had that kind.

Warm. Unhurried. The kind of voice you want to listen to even when she's saying nothing particularly important. He got lost in it every single time. Smiling without meaning to. Minutes gone before he noticed.

calling...

Vidhi
her laugh, past midnight —
the most beautiful sound

He didn't know you could miss a voice before you'd fully had it.

chapter five

And then
he saw her.

After all those nights of words and voice — he saw her face. He wasn't ready for it. Nobody could be.

Too perfect to be real. Her smile — the kind that rearranges the room. Her eyes — the kind that hold entire conversations on their own. Everything about her was textbook perfect, like someone designed a person to be precisely this wonderful and forgot to leave room for flaws. A princess. A real one.

He was already, completely, in trouble.

chapter six

Reddit to Telegram.
Riddles to real things.

The story kept expanding. What started as a thread became messages, became calls, became Instagram, became hours talking about everything and nothing — riddles and playlists and 2am thoughts and the things you only say when you actually trust someone.

Intimate conversations. The kind where you put down the performance and just exist. And it didn't feel scary with her. It felt like relief.

He reduced her cortisol. Not by trying, not by doing anything grand — just by being present. Just by being the kind of person whose company makes the noise go quiet. She felt calmer, lighter, more herself. And he had never felt so connected, so alive, so genuinely glad to know another person.

chapter seven

Some layers
still to go.

Not everything is open yet. Some of her walls are still standing — and he understands. Every wall is a story. Every lock means she's someone who knows her own worth.

And every small piece of her she's chosen to share has been more beautiful than the last. So he's not rushing. The best things don't arrive all at once. They arrive like her — slowly, on their own terms, entirely worth the wait.

He hopes those layers come down soon. Not because he needs them. Because she deserves to be fully, completely seen. And she is — already — something extraordinary.
